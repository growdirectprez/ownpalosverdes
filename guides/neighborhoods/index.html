Peninsula Neighborhood Guide

Twelve neighborhoods across four cities. Real estate data, dining, parks, trails, and what daily life actually looks like in each one. From someone who has lived here for over two decades.

Palos Verdes Estates

The historic heart of the peninsula. Master-planned in 1923 by the Olmsted Brothers with deed-controlled architecture, no commercial signage, and Mediterranean roots still visible on every block.

Palos Verdes Estates

Malaga Cove

PVE · Historic Village · Bluff Estates

The original neighborhood of Palos Verdes Estates, developed beginning in 1923 with Olmsted Brothers landscape design. Spanish Revival architecture, red clay roof tiles, and stucco facades define the streetscape. The Malaga Cove Plaza Historic District earned National Register listing in 2021. Life here centers on the plaza, the Neptune Fountain courtyard, and the bluff trails down to the cove. Quiet, historic, and deeply rooted in the peninsula's founding identity.

Daily Life

Morning coffee at Yellow Vase on the plaza patio, then a walk down the Bluff Cove Trail for ocean views. Kids at Malaga Cove Intermediate, library art shows on weekends, Friday night movies on the library lawn in summer. The PV Beach & Athletic Club has a 211-family waitlist — that tells you everything about demand here.

Lunada Bay

PVE · Coastal · Surf Culture

The most coastal of PVE's four neighborhoods. Lunada Bay sits on the western cliffs above a rocky cove known for its powerful reef break. The surf culture is real — you hear waves from the street on big swell days. Cornerstone at Pedregal is the local Montessori and Discovery-based school drawing families from across the peninsula. The neighborhood plaza anchors a small but tight-knit commercial strip. Homes range from classic ranch to custom-built cliff-edge estates with uninterrupted Pacific views.

Daily Life

Morning surf check at the bay, coffee at Lunada Kitchen, kids dropped at Cornerstone or Lunada Bay Elementary. The neighborhood is defined by its proximity to the water — surfers, tide-poolers, and dog walkers share the bluff trails daily. PVE's tree lighting ceremony, summer concerts, and library events are all within a short drive at Malaga Cove Plaza.

Valmonte

PVE · Tree-Lined · Family Core

The most popular family neighborhood in PVE. Eucalyptus-lined streets — particularly "The Grove" area — give Valmonte a dense tree canopy that feels more like a wooded retreat than a coastal suburb. No street lights, no sidewalks in most areas. Originally built in the late 1940s as modest Cape Cod and ranch homes on flat lots, many now significantly expanded or fully rebuilt. The fastest-moving market on the entire peninsula at 12 days on market.

Daily Life

Walk under the eucalyptus canopy on PV Drive North to Malaga Cove Plaza for coffee. Kids bike to Valmonte Elementary on quiet streets without sidewalks — it works because there is almost no through traffic. In 1948, the city built barriers on Via Alameda, Via Pasqual, and Via Colusa specifically to keep it that way. Summer movie nights at Ernie Howlett Park, skating in the park at the civic center in winter.

Dining & Coffee

Montemalaga

PVE · Hilltop · Panoramic Views

The highest point in PVE at 1,380 feet, Montemalaga delivers some of the most commanding ocean panoramas on the peninsula — sweeping views of the Pacific, Catalina Island, and Santa Monica Bay from Malibu to Long Beach. Strictly residential with no commercial zoning. Contains many of the largest homes on the entire peninsula, with lots among the biggest in PVE. Most new construction in PVE is happening here.

Rolling Hills Estates

Incorporated 1957. The retail and equestrian center of the peninsula. 25+ miles of dedicated bridle trails, three major shopping centers, and the only commercial districts on the Hill's western side.

Rolling Hills Estates

Rolling Hills Estates

RHE · Equestrian · Commercial Hub

The retail center of the PV Peninsula. Three major shopping centers along Silver Spur Road and Hawthorne Blvd serve the entire Hill. But the real identity is equestrian — 25+ miles of dedicated bridle trails maintained by the city, many properties zoned for horses with large lots, and the Peter Weber Equestrian Center offering boarding, lessons, and summer camps. Open streets with public access, unlike gated Rolling Hills next door.

Daily Life

Horses on the bridle trails in the morning. Coffee at Misc. Coffee or The Crepery Cafe. The Portuguese Bend National Horse Show at Ernie Howlett Park every Labor Day weekend is a 67+ year tradition. Block parties and nature walks define the social fabric.

Silver Spur

RHE · Walkable · Value Entry

The most walkable neighborhood on the Hill. Bristol Farms, the Sunday farmers market, Peninsula Shopping Center, coffee shops, and restaurants are all along Silver Spur Road — no freeway required. Predominantly single-family homes with mid-century designs, many modernized over decades. Homes move fast here: 15 days on market, strong demand from families wanting PVPUSD schools without a $3M+ entry price.

Rolling Hills

One of the few gated incorporated cities in the United States. Three staffed entry gates, private roads, no commercial zoning, and a one-acre lot minimum. About 700 homes on 2.99 square miles.

Rolling Hills

Rolling Hills

Gated City · Equestrian · Estate Living

About 700 homes across 2.99 square miles, all behind three 24/7 staffed gates. No commercial zoning, no multi-family, no sidewalks, no streetlights, no traffic signals. Every property must contain stables or reserve space for them — this is not decorative, it is a structural requirement that prevents overdevelopment and preserves the equestrian character. The third-highest median house value in the nation. Properties take the longest to sell on the peninsula at 71 days — they require the right buyer.

Daily Life

Morning trail ride on the 26 miles of maintained bridle trails. Tennis at one of the three RHCA courts. No errands within the gates — you drive five minutes to RHE for Bristol Farms, the Promenade, or Peninsula Shopping Center. Rolling Hills Country Club for golf and dining. RHCA Tennis Club tournaments, Caballeros de Rancho PV trail rides, and Women's Community Club events make up the social calendar. One of the most private residential communities in Southern California.

Rancho Palos Verdes

The largest city on the peninsula by area. Incorporated in 1973 to maintain low-density residential character. Home to Terranea Resort, Point Vicente Lighthouse, and 1,600+ acres of nature preserves.

Rancho Palos Verdes

Mira Catalina

RPV · Elevated · Catalina Views

Named for its panoramic Catalina Island views. About 758 homes at some of the highest elevations on the peninsula, dating to 1958. Quiet, family-friendly, low turnover — only 14 sales in all of 2025, making it one of the tightest markets on the Hill. Many homes also overlook the LA basin and harbor. Feels removed from urban LA despite being 30 minutes from downtown via the 110.

Daily Life

Catalina Island visible from your kitchen window on clear mornings. Hike the 123-acre Deane Dana Friendship Park with harbor and island views. Summer concerts at Ken Dyda Civic Center on Saturday afternoons, Shakespeare by the Sea at Hesse Park in July. Trump National Golf Club is adjacent, Los Verdes Golf Course is nearby and public. Terranea Resort is the closest luxury amenity anchor — spa, restaurants, oceanfront trails.

Los Verdes

RPV · Golf Course · Ocean Bluffs

Built around the Los Verdes Golf Course on the western bluffs. Ranch-style homes from the 1960s–70s, many with ocean views from Malibu to Catalina. No HOA, no monthly dues. The golf course itself is one of the top 10 busiest public courses in the nation — 18 holes designed by William Bell with panoramic ocean views from most holes. Not in the active landslide zone. Accessible entry price with strong demand at $1.75M median.

PV Drive East

RPV · Sea Cliffs · Volume Market

The largest MLS area in RPV by sales volume — 48 closings in 2025. Runs along the eastern side of the peninsula above dramatic sea cliffs. About 1,943 homes, averaging 2,819 sq ft on spacious lots. Mid-range pricing for the peninsula at $1.94M median. Terranea Resort is the luxury amenity anchor. Semi-rural feel with large lots, equestrian-friendly areas, and no sidewalks on many streets. The city was incorporated in 1973 specifically to maintain this character.

La Cresta

RPV · Estate Lots · Premium Views

Estate-oriented neighborhood on elevated ground in RPV. About 1,125 homes, averaging 3,511 sq ft on larger-than-average lots. Purely residential with no commercial zoning — the nearest shops are at Golden Cove Shopping Center and the Promenade. Sweeping Pacific and Catalina views from the elevated position. Not in the landslide zone. Properties take longer to sell at 47 days — typical for estate-scale real estate that requires the right match.

San Pedro (Peninsula-Adjacent)

South Shores

San Pedro · Coastal · Value Entry

Southern edge of the peninsula, technically City of Los Angeles (San Pedro) bordering RPV. The most affordable peninsula entry at $1.35M median and the fastest-moving market at 17 days. Mid-century coastal homes on wide streets with unobstructable Pacific and Catalina views. Two tiers: Lower South Shores closer to the water with coastal trail access, Upper South Shores at higher elevation with wider views. SSCA (South Shores Community Association) has been active for 50+ years. Important note: schools are LAUSD, not PVPUSD.

Daily Life

Walk to Averill Park with its gazebo, artificial pond, and harbor views. Dinner at Shore Grille — the neighborhood's own scratch kitchen. CRAFTED at the Port of LA for year-round artisan goods. San Pedro's waterfront district is undergoing real growth — restaurants, galleries, film festivals.
