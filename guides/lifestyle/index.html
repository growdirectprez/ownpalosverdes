Life on the Hill

Golf courses on ocean cliffs. Horses on bridle trails through coastal sage. Four hundred acres of open reserve land a mile from your front door. This is what daily life looks like on the Palos Verdes Peninsula.

History & Heritage

A Planned Community Since 1913

The Vanderlip Vision

In 1913, New York banker Frank Vanderlip purchased 16,000 acres of the Palos Verdes Peninsula with a vision for a planned residential community. He hired the Olmsted Brothers — sons of Central Park's designer — to lay out the landscape plan. Development began in earnest in the 1920s, with deed-controlled Spanish Colonial architecture, preserved ridgeline views, and wide setbacks that still define the peninsula's character today.

The original Malaga Cove Plaza, completed in 1926, was the commercial anchor — a Mediterranean courtyard with the Neptune Fountain carved from Italian marble. It earned National Register of Historic Places listing in 2021.

What the Planning Preserved

The Olmsted plan prioritized open space, ocean views, and natural contours over grid-pattern subdivision. That decision still shapes daily life. More than half the peninsula is open space, parkland, or preserve. There are no billboards. Commercial signage is regulated. The ridgeline is protected.

Portuguese Bend's Riding Club dates to the Vanderlip era — originally the stables for Villa Narcissa, the family estate. Rolling Hills incorporated as a gated city in 1957 specifically to preserve its horse-zoned, rural character. These weren't accidental outcomes. They were planned.

The Palos Verdes Community Archives at palosverdeshistory.org maintains the primary collection of historical photos, maps, and records from the peninsula's development.

Trails & Open Space

Four Hundred Acres of Reserve, Twelve Miles of Coast

More than half the peninsula is undeveloped. That's not an accident — it's the direct result of the 1920s Olmsted plan and decades of land-use decisions that prioritized open space over density. The result is a trail network that runs from the ridgeline to the ocean, through coastal sage scrub, grassland, and rocky bluffs.

Portuguese Bend Reserve

The largest contiguous open space on the peninsula — over 400 acres with 12 named trails for hiking, mountain biking, and equestrian use. The terrain is coastal sage scrub and grassland with ocean views from the ridgeline. Trailheads off Crenshaw Boulevard and Del Cerro Park. Connects to Forrestal Nature Reserve to the south.

Coastal Preserves

  • PV Shoreline Preserve — 4.5-mile bluff trail, whale watching Nov–Apr
  • Forrestal Nature Reserve — 137 acres, connects to Portuguese Bend
  • Abalone Cove Ecological Reserve — tide pools, sea caves, kelp beds
  • Point Vicente Interpretive Center — whale migration lookout, native plant gardens

Gardens & Parks

  • South Coast Botanic Garden — 87 acres on a reclaimed mine site, 2,500+ plant species
  • Point Vicente Lighthouse — 1926, still operational, open first Saturday monthly
  • Founders Park (PVE) — tennis, playground, Catalina views
  • Hesse Park (RPV) — community programs, gardens, event space
  • Ernie Howlett Park (RHE) — baseball, soccer, equestrian arena
Golf

Five Courses, Three Tiers

Five courses spanning private, daily-fee, and public — from generational-membership clubs to ocean-view county greens.

Palos Verdes Golf Club

Private, member-only. George C. Thomas design, opened 1924 — one of the original community amenities planned alongside Malaga Cove. Classic Southern California parkland course with ocean views and a modest clubhouse. Membership carries lineage weight in PVE circles, particularly in Valmonte, Malaga Cove, and Lunada Bay.

Rolling Hills Country Club

Private, full-service country club in Rolling Hills Estates. Golf, pool, tennis, fitness, dining, and events. Recent course renovation investment. Family-oriented — juniors, swim team, tennis lessons. The default club for RHE and Rolling Hills families.

Los Verdes Golf Course

LA County-operated public course at 7000 W Los Verdes Drive, RPV. Eighteen holes with genuine ocean and cliff views at county public rates. Double-deck driving range. Casual bar and cafe open to walk-ins. The clubhouse terrace hosts weddings and events at a fraction of resort pricing — a genuine local-favorite venue. For buyers used to private-club minimums, the honest message here is: you can just play on a Tuesday.

Trump National Golf Club Los Angeles

Daily-fee at premium rates. Located on the coastal cliffs adjacent to Terranea. Originally the Pete Dye-designed Ocean Trails course; Trump purchased after the 1999 landslide collapse and rebuilt with a $250M restoration, reopening January 2006. Dramatic cliff and ocean views. Heavy event and wedding business.

The Links at Terranea

Nine-hole oceanfront par-3 course at Terranea Resort, 100 Terranea Way, RPV. Public daily-fee. Short, walkable, dramatic ocean bluff setting with a practice area and multi-tiered short-game facility. Not a championship course — a lifestyle course. Good for practice rounds, walking nine with friends, or introducing beginners.

Terranea Memberships

For residents who don't want a private club commitment, Terranea offers a three-part membership stack: the Links Card for golf access, a Spa membership for wellness and pool access, and a Palos Verdes Local Resident rate (from ~$325/night, complimentary parking, waived resort fee). For many Hill families, Terranea functions as a de facto country club without the initiation.

Equestrian

Horses on the Hill

Horses are part of the zoning in Rolling Hills — private stables, direct trail access, and a real riding community.

Portuguese Bend Riding Club

Ten-plus acres on Narcissa Drive in the gated Portuguese Bend community. Full-service barn with boarding, lessons, and training across all disciplines. Originally the stables for Villa Narcissa — Frank and Narcissa Vanderlip's 1920s estate. Nearing its 100th year. One of the very first structures built on the Hill.

Empty Saddle Club

Thirteen acres in Rolling Hills Estates. Private member organization focused on preserving and promoting Western riding traditions. Member shows, clinics, social events, and junior programs. The cultural anchor of RHE's equestrian identity.

Palos Verdes Stables

Three-plus acres in Palos Verdes Estates, established 1926. Full-service boarding and lessons across all disciplines and experience levels. Direct access to the PVE trail network. The PVE-side counterpart to PBRC.

Trails for Riders

Portuguese Bend Reserve and Forrestal Nature Reserve offer extensive multi-use trails through coastal sage scrub — open to equestrian use. Rolling Hills maintains a private residents-only trail system tied directly to property value. The PVE trail network provides scenic routes from PV Stables and the Malaga Cove side.

Ernie Howlett Park

The public equestrian arena in Rolling Hills Estates, shared with baseball and soccer fields. Hosts clinics, local shows, and recreational rides. Arguably the most active public equestrian venue on the Hill — a high-traffic Saturday destination. For families new to horses, Howlett is the accessible entry point.

Beaches & Coast

Rocky Coves, Tide Pools, and Open Water

Rocky coves, tide pools, and sea caves along 12 miles of shoreline — accessed by trails, not boardwalks.

Abalone Cove

Part of the Abalone Cove Ecological Reserve. Rocky shoreline with accessible tide pools, sea caves, and kelp beds. The most-visited cove on the peninsula. Parking lot and trail access off Palos Verdes Drive South. Docent-led tide pool programs run seasonally.

Lunada Bay

A rocky cove below PVE's western cliffs, known for its powerful reef break. Serious surf spot — heavy swell, experienced surfers only. You can hear the waves from the street on big days. The bluff-top views are accessible to anyone via the coastal trail.

Bluff Cove

Below Malaga Cove, accessed via the Bluff Cove Trail (1.7-mile coastal bluff walk from Malaga Cove Plaza). Rocky shoreline with tide pools and diving access. A morning walk down and back up is the default Malaga Cove routine.

RAT Beach

At the base of PVE where the peninsula meets Torrance Beach. The name stands for Right After Torrance. Surfing, diving, kelp forest. One of the few sand-accessible beaches on the peninsula side, connecting to the Torrance Beach bike path and the Marvin Braude trail heading north.

Point Vicente

Point Vicente Lighthouse has stood on the bluff since 1926 and remains operational. The Point Vicente Interpretive Center is the primary whale-watching site on the peninsula — gray whale migration runs November through April. Native plant demonstration gardens surround the center.

Torrance Beach

At the base of the peninsula where PVE meets Torrance. A wide, flat sand beach with the Marvin Braude bike path, volleyball courts, and the Strand running north to Hermosa and Manhattan Beach. Where Hill families go when they want an actual beach day without driving to the South Bay beach cities.

Arts & Culture

Art Centers, Theater, and Garden Shows

Palos Verdes Art Center

The peninsula's primary visual arts institution, located on Crestridge Road in RPV. Rotating gallery exhibitions, studio classes for all ages, ceramics and printmaking studios, and an annual art show and sale. Community-supported, volunteer-driven. The Art Center functions as the cultural anchor for the Hill's visual arts scene.

Norris Theatre

A 450-seat performing arts theater at the Norris Center in Rolling Hills Estates (27570 Crossfield Drive). Professional touring productions, local theater companies, concerts, and community performances. One of the few dedicated performing arts venues between the South Bay beach cities and Long Beach. Shows run year-round.

Malaga Cove Library

The original 1930 library building in Malaga Cove Plaza. Hosts gallery exhibitions, author events, and the summer reading program. The building itself is a contributing structure to the Malaga Cove Plaza National Register Historic District. Friday night movies on the library lawn run through summer.

South Coast Botanic Garden

Eighty-seven acres on a reclaimed diatomaceous earth mine in RPV. Over 2,500 plant species across themed gardens. Hosts seasonal exhibitions, outdoor concerts, plant sales, and the popular holiday lights event. A membership here is one of the most-used family amenities on the peninsula — it's a default weekend walk.

Dining & Village Life

Commercial Pockets, Not Strips

No downtown — commercial life clusters in small, walkable pockets, each with its own character.

Malaga Cove Plaza

The original commercial center, built 1926. Yellow Vase (French-American brunch, in-house bakery), Malaga Cove Ranch Market (deli, bakery, wine bar since 1952), The Cove Cafe (gourmet coffee, flatbreads). The Neptune Fountain courtyard anchors weekend strolling. The plaza is walkable from Malaga Cove and Valmonte.

Silver Spur Corridor

The commercial spine of Rolling Hills Estates, running along Silver Spur Road. Peninsula Center is the primary shopping center (Trader Joe's, restaurants, services). This is where most Hill families do their daily errands. Compass's local office is here at 550 Silver Spur Road.

Terranea Resort

Multiple dining venues at the resort — from casual poolside to fine dining at mar'sel. Terranea functions as the peninsula's de facto resort dining destination. The Palos Verdes Local Resident rate makes it accessible for staycations. Spa, pools, and the Links course round out the day-use picture.

Riviera Village

Technically in Redondo Beach, but a five-minute drive from the base of the peninsula and functionally the Hill's closest walkable restaurant and shopping district. Boutiques, restaurants, coffee shops, and bars along Catalina Avenue. Friday farmers' market. Most Hill families consider Riviera Village part of their regular dining rotation.

Sports & Recreation

A Total Industry for All Sports

Rec leagues, travel ball, and an unusual density of organized programs for a community this size.

Baseball & Softball

Four Little League charters serve the peninsula — Palos Verdes LL, Silver Spur LL (Ernie Howlett Park), Lunada Bay LL, and PV Peninsula Junior League (up to age 14, full 90-foot diamond). Four charters on a peninsula this size is a tell: PV is a baseball town. Travel programs plug into the broader South Bay circuit.

Soccer

AYSO Region 10 is the default entry point. Year-round, ages U5 through U19, 100% volunteer-run. The vast majority of Hill kids who play soccer start here. Families seeking higher-level play move to South Bay club programs — Beach FC, South Bay Force, and Galaxy-affiliated clubs, with practices off-Hill.

Football & Cheer

PV Pop Warner (the Mavericks) is the only tackle football and cheer program on the peninsula. Fifty-plus years serving the Hill, ages 5–14, flag through tackle. The cheer program competes at conference, regional, and national Pop Warner championships. Peninsula and San Pedro catchment.

Volleyball

Arguably the biggest travel-ball commitment for Hill girls. Mizuno Long Beach, Surfside Volleyball Club, and other major South Bay clubs draw significant PV contingents. Practices off-Hill. Tournament travel is regional and national. Both PV High and Peninsula High field competitive programs that feed (and are fed by) the club pipeline.

Swimming & Water Polo

The PV Beach & Athletic Club competition pool anchors a strong summer-league and year-round swim culture. Both high schools field competitive water polo programs supported by South Bay club water polo. Year-round competitive swimming through South Bay swim clubs.

Tennis

The peninsula has a deep tennis culture anchored by four clubs. Palos Verdes Tennis Club (3303 Via Campesina, PVE) is private with 11 lighted courts, 8 pickleball courts, a Junior Tennis Academy open to non-members (ages 3–16, USTA curriculum), and league play. Peninsula Racquet Club (30850 Hawthorne Blvd, RPV) has 12 LED-lit courts with ocean and Catalina views — equity-owned, non-profit. Jack Kramer Club (11 Montecillo Dr, RHE) has 13 courts, a gym, pool, and a legendary junior pipeline — founded in 1962 with Vic Braden, it trained Tracy Austin, Pete Sampras, and Lindsay Davenport. Ernie Howlett Park (25851 Hawthorne, RHE) has 8 public courts open daily. Public courts also at Eastview Park (RPV), Malaga Cove (PVE), and George F Canyon (RHE). Several HOA communities maintain private courts. And if you're shopping for a home with its own court, the peninsula has them — particularly in Rolling Hills, RHE, and the larger-lot RPV neighborhoods. Ask me which listings have courts.

Pickleball

Pickleball has exploded on the Hill. PV Tennis Club has 8 dedicated lighted courts with weekly open play ($15 PVE residents, $20 non-residents, free for members). Peninsula Racquet Club has 4 courts ($49–69/month pickleball membership). The city of RPV added 2 courts at the Ken Dyda Civic Center — free, first-come first-served. Ernie Howlett Park in RHE has pickleball overlays with $5 drop-in play and free Fridays.

Surfing & Lacrosse

PV Surf team has been historically competitive — centered on Lunada Bay, RAT Beach, and the coves. PV Lacrosse club programs feed both high school teams with travel pipelines for serious players.

Rec vs. Travel: The Real Commitment

A family doing AYSO plus PVBA basketball is maybe 4–6 hours per week. A family with one kid in travel volleyball and a sibling in travel baseball is routinely 20-plus hours per week, plus weekend tournament travel and $5,000–$15,000+ in annual team fees. The gap in time and money between rec-league and travel-ball is meaningful. Worth understanding before you buy into a neighborhood based on field proximity.

The Saturday Morning Hub

For families with elementary-age kids, Saturdays at Ernie Howlett Park become a de facto community event. Little League on three fields, soccer on three more, equestrian in the arena. Several neighborhoods — RHE, the Crest, adjacent RPV — sell the "bike to Howlett" story. City rec programs in RPV (Hesse Park), PVE (Farnham Martin Park), and RHE (Ernie Howlett) provide lower-barrier entry before families commit to a league.

Fitness & Wellness

Gyms, Studios & Training

Equinox, boutique studios, and budget gyms clustered along the Deep Valley corridor in Rolling Hills Estates.

Equinox Palos Verdes

550 Deep Valley Dr, Rolling Hills Estates. The luxury option — pool, spa, kids club, cycling and group fitness studios. Open Mon–Thu 5am–10pm, Fri 5am–8pm. The default for Hill residents who want a full-service premium gym.

Coreology

704 Deep Valley Dr, Rolling Hills Estates. Boutique studio — pilates-inspired XFormer reformer classes, sculpt, treadmill intervals, and indoor cycling. Small group classes, musically driven, with personalized instructor attention.

Fitness 19

51 Peninsula Center, Rolling Hills Estates. Budget-friendly gym with standard equipment and machines. No frills, gets the job done.

PV Beach & Athletic Club

389 Paseo Del Mar, PVE. Five-story clifftop clubhouse with a competition 6-lane pool, children’s pool, and fitness center. The swim culture anchor for the Hill — summer league and year-round programs. Coach Ken Taylor runs junior lifeguard prep classes here.

24 Hour Fitness

No location on the Hill proper. Closest: Rolling Hills Plaza at 2685 PCH, Torrance and the Del Amo Super-Sport at 21501 Hawthorne Blvd, Torrance. Both a 10–15 minute drive from the peninsula.

Beach & Outdoor

Beach bootcamp programs run regularly at Torrance Beach and RAT Beach. CorePower Yoga has a Torrance studio (closest to the Hill). Orangetheory Fitness in both Redondo Beach and Torrance. Trail running on the Palos Verdes Estates Shoreline Preserve and the Portuguese Bend Reserve. No CrossFit box on the Hill — closest in Redondo and Torrance.

Frozen Treats

Ice Cream on the Hill

Three new shops opened on the Hill in 2024–2025, each anchoring a different village pocket.

Lunada Creamery

711 Yarmouth Rd, PVE — in Lunada Bay Plaza. Soft serve with weekly rotating flavors, always at least one vegan option, homemade cookie sandwiches, and take-home pints. Founded by Julie and Jason Anthony, who moved to PV and spent 15 months developing the concept. Minimalist 380-square-foot space in the former ATM spot. Mon–Thu 12–8, Fri 12–9.

Neptune’s Frozen Treats

55 Malaga Cove Plaza, PVE. Frozen yogurt, custard, gelato, and sorbet with rotating flavors. Founded by Katherine Kemps, a PV native whose great-great-grandfather started Kemps Ice Cream in the Midwest and Beverly Dairy in LA. Coastal décor with a mosaic Neptune logo wall. Sun–Thu 12–8, Fri–Sat 12–9.

Golden Scoop Gelato

31228 Palos Verdes Dr W, RPV — Golden Cove Center at Hawthorne. Twenty-four flavors of small-batch all-natural gelato (Earl Grey, pistachio, salted caramel), plus affogato, soft serve, and croffles. Real California Milk, no artificial ingredients. Founded by Nathan Kang. Mon–Fri 12–9, Sat–Sun 11–9.

Handel’s — San Pedro

970 N Western Ave, San Pedro. Homemade recipes since 1945 — 45-plus flavors made fresh daily on premises. $2.50 scoop Tuesdays. Named one of the Ten Best Ice Creams by National Geographic. Daily 12–9:30pm.

Handel’s — Redondo Beach

1882 Pacific Coast Highway, Redondo Beach. Same 45-plus daily flavors, cones, sundaes, shakes, and pops. The South Bay anchor since 2010. Daily 11am–10pm.

Kids & Summer

Junior Guards, Surf Camp & Everything Else

Junior guards, surf camp, Little League, dance, and the coaches and tutors that fill the after-school calendar.

LA County Junior Lifeguards

The defining summer experience for Hill kids. Run by LA County Fire Department — the same lifeguards who work the beaches. Ages 9–17, six weeks (June–August), Monday–Thursday 9am–3pm. Torrance Beach is the main South Bay station. Kids do ocean swimming, body surfing, paddleboard, surf rescue, first aid, beach runs, and physical conditioning. Cost is roughly $685 including uniform. Returning JGs register in February; new applicants must pass a swim test (100 yards — 1:50 for ages 9–11, 1:40 for 12–13, 1:30 for 14–17). JG is a rite of passage — kids come back every summer, parents organize around the schedule, and it’s the connective tissue between Hill families at the beach.

Training for the JG Swim Test

Many kids need coaching before they can pass. Coach Ken Taylor at PV Beach & Athletic Club is the go-to — he’s been the JG prep instructor since 1996, runs three classes a day through spring, and almost all his students pass. Other options: South End Racquet Club (ZAP Swimming), Swimphi (private lessons tailored to the test), South Bay Swim Team (JG prep), and Waterworks Swim School in Torrance (JG Prep courses). LA County also offers free JG Ready Training — technique sessions with lifeguard staff before tryouts.

Surf Camps & Lessons

PV Surf Camp (300 Paseo Del Mar, PVE) is the local standard — ages 6–14, weekly sessions June–August, $389/week, 4:1 instructor ratio, run by Leith Emery. Torrance Beach is the go-to for lessons (broad takeoff zone, consistent whitewater). Redondo Surf Club offers private coaching (max 4 students). Surf With Joey does private lessons in PV and Malibu. Dive N’ Surf runs year-round private lessons, ages 4–84.

Swim Coaching

Beyond JG prep, the Hill has strong year-round swim options. South Bay Swim Team for competitive year-round swimming. SwimmingWithAnnie covers PV, Torrance, and Redondo (private, semi-private, group). Swim 310 does house-to-house private lessons across the peninsula. SafeSplash Torrance and Waterworks (Torrance) handle beginner through competitive. PV Beach & Athletic Club competition pool anchors summer league.

Summer Camps

Rolling Hills Country Day Camp (ages 4½–13) — swimming, arts, sports, nature, plus academic enrichment. PBC Day Camp (ages 4–14) at Rolling Hills Prep campus — 30 summers, ACA-accredited, 20 acres. Specialty camps: Code Ninjas (coding), School of Rock PV (music), Urban Youth Park (parkour), TennisPower at Peninsula Racquet Club. PVPUSD runs district summer programs.

Dance & Performing Arts

Palos Verdes Ballet (RHE) — the premier ballet school in South Bay since 1955. Classical ballet, contemporary, pointe, summer intensive. Peninsula School of Performing Arts — 30-plus years of dance for kids and adults. Elite Dance Studio (RHE) — tap, ballet, jazz, hip hop, tumble in small group classes.

Little League & Rec Sports

Four Little League charters on the peninsula: Palos Verdes LL, Silver Spur LL (Ernie Howlett Park), Rolling Hills LL, and PV Peninsula Junior League. AYSO Region 10 for soccer. PV Pop Warner (Mavericks) for tackle football and cheer. Spring Training Baseball Camp (Dan Spring) is the local institution — a former Brown captain and Detroit Tigers draft pick who’s coached 15,000-plus kids over 15 years in PV. Sixty-plus private lessons a week, 11 weeks of summer camp, fall league. Relentlessly positive culture — no tryouts, no negativity. City rec programs at Hesse Park (RPV), Farnham Martin Park (PVE), and Ernie Howlett (RHE) provide entry-level access before committing to a league.

Coaching & Tutoring

Private tennis coaching through the clubs or on public courts — PVTC Junior Academy is open to non-members. Bob Krueger (USPTA certified) teaches at Ernie Howlett. For academics: Peninsula Academy (since 1990, tutoring and college counseling), Blue Train College Prep (SAT/ACT, works with athletic recruits), Study Hut Tutoring (PV and beach cities), PV Prep Tutoring. The student-athlete pipeline is real — coaches and tutors here understand the scheduling demands.
