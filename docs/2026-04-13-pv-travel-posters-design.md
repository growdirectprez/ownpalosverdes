# Palos Verdes Neighborhood Travel Posters — Design Spec

> **Date:** 2026-04-13
> **Purpose:** AI image generation prompts for 1920s/1930s vintage travel poster art, one per PV Peninsula neighborhood
> **Usage:** OwnPalosVerdes.com neighborhood cards, section headers, print collateral
> **Style reference:** Art Deco travel posters (Roger Broders, A.M. Cassandre, Tom Purvis) — bold flat colors, geometric simplification, dramatic perspectives, hand-lettered typography feel

---

## Shared Style Direction

**Reference:** Modern National Park travel poster style (see Anderson Design Group, Yosemite series). Rich, saturated, illustrative — NOT minimal geometric Art Deco. These feel like paintings you'd frame, not diagrams.

All posters share these traits:
- **Style:** Vintage National Park travel poster — rich illustrative landscapes with saturated color, dramatic lighting, painterly depth. Warm and inviting, not cold and geometric.
- **Technique:** Layered landscape illustration with atmospheric depth. Bold saturated colors, soft gradients in sky, detailed foreground foliage, mid-ground landmark, distant horizon. Feels hand-painted, not vector.
- **Composition:** Strong vertical format (2:3 ratio), dramatic landscape fills 70-80% of canvas, bold title text integrated at top or bottom with a banner/frame treatment
- **Typography:** Bold serif or slab-serif neighborhood name at top, "PALOS VERDES PENINSULA · CALIFORNIA" subtitle at bottom in smaller caps. Text feels carved/stamped, not floating.
- **Color intensity:** Deep saturated tones — ocean blues, sunset oranges, forest greens, golden hour warmth. Richer than reality. Each poster has a dominant color mood.
- **Lighting:** Golden hour or dramatic sky in every poster — sunrise, sunset, cloud drama. The sky tells a story.
- **Border treatment:** Thin cream/ivory border with rounded corners, like a framed print. Optional dark wood frame effect for web display.
- **Mood:** "You belong here" — aspirational but warm, the kind of poster you'd buy at a gift shop and hang in your home
- **Details:** Trees, flowers, native plants rendered with care. Architecture simplified but recognizable. No people, but the scene feels lived-in.

## Production Notes

### Image Generation

- **Target size:** 1200x1600px (3:4 portrait) for web; 2400x3200px for print
- **Format:** PNG with transparency option (for overlay on dark backgrounds)
- **AI tools:** Midjourney, DALL-E 3, or Ideogram — test with "vintage 1930s travel poster, Art Deco style, flat color lithograph" as style prefix
- **Consistency:** Generate 3–4 variants per neighborhood, pick the strongest, then re-run with refinements
- **Typography:** Generate without text, add neighborhood name in post-production with a consistent period-appropriate typeface (Futura, Kabel, Broadway, or Peignot)

### Typeface Recommendations

| Font | Use | Why |
|------|-----|-----|
| **Futura Bold** | Neighborhood names | The quintessential Art Deco geometric sans |
| **Kabel** | Taglines | Softer geometric, warm period feel |
| **Broadway** | "Own Palos Verdes" header | Distinctly 1920s, instantly recognizable |
| **Peignot** | Alternative accent | French Art Deco, luxury feel |
